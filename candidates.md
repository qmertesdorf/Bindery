# 10 candidate projects + validation gate

Derived from the verified strategy (Broad Category + Specific Audience + Clear Benefit;
avoid all confirmed dead zones; build a series in ONE niche). **None are backed by hard
sales data** — they are structurally sound starting points that MUST pass the validation
gate below before any production work.

## Low-content / medium-content (fastest, cheapest — design tools only)
1. **Profession-specific clinical/field logbooks** — e.g. *Nurse Practitioner Clinical
   Rotation Logbook*, *Phlebotomy Competency Log*, *HVAC Service Call Logbook*. Real
   required-tracking fields = genuine utility, narrow competition. (The verified example.)
2. **Sobriety / recovery milestone trackers** — day-count, trigger journaling,
   gratitude-on-hard-days. Identity-driven, giftable, emotionally motivated buyers.
3. **Shift-worker / first-responder planners** — built for rotating shifts (nurses,
   police, firefighters, pilots) that standard weekly planners handle badly.
4. **Beekeeping / homestead / specialty-hobby logs** — hive inspection logs, chicken-flock
   records, sourdough bake logs. Purpose-built record books; tiny competitor pools.
5. **Large-print specialty puzzle/activity books for a defined audience** — e.g.
   *Large-Print Word Search for Memory Care*, or themed by hobby/region. (Generic puzzles
   saturated; theme + format + audience combo is not.)
6. **Pet-specific health & care logbooks** — *Senior Dog Health Journal*, *Reptile
   Husbandry Log*, *New Puppy First-Year Record*. Emotional, giftable, species-specific fields.

## Medium / high-content nonfiction (more work, higher per-unit royalty, better moat)
7. **Hyper-local / hyper-specific how-to guides** — *Container Gardening in the Pacific
   Northwest*, *Beginner Beekeeping in Cold Climates*. Local specificity is hard to
   AI-spam and dodges the generic how-to dead zone.
8. **Caregiver / life-transition practical guides** — *Caring for a Parent with Dementia
   (First-Timer's Guide)*, *Downsizing After 60*. High emotional need, evergreen,
   underserved vs. generic self-help.
9. **Niche skill workbooks with exercises** — *Mediterranean Meal-Prep Workbook for Busy
   Families*, *Phlebotomy Certification Practice Workbook*. Workbooks justify higher
   prices and resist commoditization (interactivity > prose).
10. **Children's books for an underserved specific situation** — e.g. a parent deployed
    overseas, starting a new school mid-year, a sibling with a specific condition.
    Specific emotional gap; AI-assisted illustration + tightly human-edited story.

## The non-negotiable validation gate (run BEFORE writing anything)

For each candidate:
- **Live competition check** — search the exact term on Amazon. Goldilocks zone:
  enough results that demand clearly exists, but top sellers have mediocre covers / low
  review counts you can beat. Zero results usually = zero buyers, not a goldmine.
- **Confirm demand** — keyword tool (Publisher Rocket; Book Bolt for low-content) or
  Amazon autocomplete + best-seller rank of the top 3 results.
- **Start with 2–3 titles in ONE validated niche, not 10 scattered** — the catalog-size
  effect comes from series depth; lets you reuse covers, interiors, and ad keywords.

## Expectation-setting (from verified data)
Typical outcome is small (≤$100/mo for most). Success correlates with patiently building
a catalog + spending on ads. No credible evidence of passive riches. Frame as a
slow-build side business where a few validated winners eventually carry a growing catalog.
