# Research findings — AI books on Amazon KDP

Two deep-research passes (fan-out web search + 3-vote adversarial verification),
run 2026-06-05. Only claims that survived verification are presented as findings.
Refuted claims are listed separately — **do not rely on them**.

---

## 1. KDP policy on AI content (HIGH confidence — Amazon primary docs)

- **AI is permitted, but disclosure is mandatory.** You must privately tell Amazon
  when content is **AI-GENERATED** (text, images, or translations created by an AI
  tool) at publish/republish time — **even if you heavily edited it afterward.**
- **AI-ASSISTED** (you wrote it; AI only refined/error-checked/improved) → **no
  disclosure required.**
- Disclosure is an internal yes/no on the dashboard. **Buyers do NOT see it** ("at
  this time" — Amazon reserves the right to change that).
- Books are **not** rejected merely for being AI-generated if disclosed and compliant.
- Sources: kdp.amazon.com Content Guidelines (G200672390); Authors Guild; Publishers Weekly.

## 2. Royalty economics (HIGH confidence — Amazon primary docs, current 2026)

**Kindle ebooks:**
- **70% royalty ONLY if priced $2.99–$9.99 (USD).** Outside that band → **35%.**
- At 70%, Amazon also subtracts a **delivery fee of $0.15/MB per copy** (~$0.82 for a
  5.49 MB file). 35% tier has no delivery fee.
- Source: kdp.amazon.com G200634560, G200634500.

**Print paperbacks** — royalty = **(rate × list price) − printing cost**:
- **60%** if list price ≥ $9.99
- **50%** if below $9.99 — *dropped from 60%, effective June 10, 2025*
- **40%** via expanded distribution
- Source: kdp.amazon.com G201834330 (+ KDP Community forum on the June 2025 change).
- NOTE: a specific worked royalty example ("$3/sale", "$7.54 on a $19.99 book") was
  **refuted** — compute per-book with Amazon's printing-cost calculator.

## 3. Earnings reality (HIGH confidence — primary survey data)

- Written Word Media 2025 survey (n=1,346, *marketing-active* indies): **44% earn
  ≤$100/month; only 8% earn >$10k/month.** Sample self-selects for serious authors,
  so the true beginner baseline is worse.
- Authors Guild 2023 (2022 data, n=5,699): **median book income ~$2,000/year**; median
  total author income ~$5,000.
- **Full-time** self-published authors: **~$12,800 median** (Authors Guild; ALLi $12,749).
- Romance ~ double the all-genre median (~$31,725).

## 4. The three verified income levers

1. **Catalog size = strongest single income predictor.** ~80% of authors with 1–3
   books earn <$100/mo; authors with 25+ books have a ~$3,000/mo median; the >$20k/mo
   bracket averages ~61 books. → **volume/series game, not a one-book bet.**
   (Correlational — confounders co-vary; not proven causal.)
2. **Marketing spend scales sharply with earnings.** >$10k/mo earners spend ~$4,500/mo
   on marketing vs ~$81/mo for the bottom tier (all-respondent avg ~$636/mo).
   → **budget for Amazon Ads; don't expect organic-only to work.** (Correlational.)
3. **Niche down.** Winning formula echoed across all sources:
   **Broad Category + Specific Audience + Clear Benefit.** A "Nurse Practitioner
   Clinical Rotation Logbook" with real tracking fields wins where a plain lined
   notebook dies. Must be demand-validated ("Goldilocks zone") — too narrow = no buyers.

## 5. Saturated dead zones to AVOID (HIGH confidence)

Plain notebooks/journals, generic self-help, generic weight loss, crypto-for-beginners,
dropshipping/FBA courses, generic productivity, social-media marketing, generic
business/how-to guides, motivational titles, and "make money with AI" books. AI
flooding has worsened all of these.

## 6. Risk / enforcement (HIGH confidence on rules; mechanics from blogs)

- **~3 new title uploads/day per account** since Sept 2023 (anti-abuse). Established
  quality accounts can get higher caps/exemptions.
- Non-disclosure + rapid-fire low-quality/duplicate publishing → spam detection →
  title suppression → removal → **account suspension/termination**. Reactive mass
  de-listings have happened (e.g., 2023 AI mushroom-foraging guide removals).
- **Key nuance:** volume alone is fine (legit 200+ book publishers operate normally);
  it's **velocity + low quality + non-disclosure** that triggers bans.
- The exact "submission-velocity risk-scoring algorithm" is from third-party blogs,
  not an Amazon primary doc — directionally true, not officially specified.

## REFUTED claims — do NOT trust (failed adversarial verification)

- Specific income distributions ("41% under $250/mo, 6% over $10k/mo").
- Low-content monthly income ranges ("$500–$3,000/mo after 50–100 titles",
  "$50–$300/mo per book").
- Worked royalty/print-cost examples ("$3/sale", "~$2.15 print cost", "$7.54 on $19.99").
- "$13,500 median self-published income."
- Named nonfiction sub-niches with exact search volumes & margins (e.g. "Personal
  Finance for Solopreneurs ~18K searches, 72% margin").
- "Planners/fitness/gratitude journals are the most profitable low-content categories."
- "Coloring books / large-print senior puzzles are the safest profitable niches."
- "Catalog size predicts income more reliably than ANY other factor" (overreach;
  primary source says "one of the strongest").

**Takeaway:** any precise dollar figure from a KDP-tool marketing blog should be
treated as unverified marketing until checked against live Amazon data.
